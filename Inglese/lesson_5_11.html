English - Lesson 5/11

  • Future Forms:
  • For certainty:
    1. Present Simple -> Schedules. Example: The train leaves at 4 pm on Friday;
    2. Present Continous -> Arrangements. Example: I'm meeting her at 9 pm at the restaurant;
    3. Be going to + base form (most common form) -> plans. Example: I'm going to/gonna have dinner out tomorrow;
    4. Will -> Spontaneous ideas for the future. Example: I think I'll stay home this evening;
    5. May/Might -> uncertain possibility. Example: I might go to the cinema, or I might watch TV;
  • For predictions:
    1. Will -> only an opinion;
    2. Be going to -> when you have knowledge or expertise, or when there is visible evidence to base the prediction;

  • Articles
  • Indefinite article:
    1. a, an -> use "a" before a consonant sound, "an" before a vowel sound. Example: A uniform; a union; an article;
  • Definite article:
    1. The -> 2 pronunciations: long (before a vowel sound) and short (before a consonant sound)
  • No article: generalizing/abstractions/institutions/set expressions. Examples: Dogs are my favourite snack/Art is the supreme form of living/He is in hospital/I go to bed at 11pm;
  • Musical instruments take definite article. Example: I play the guitar;
  • Natural items:
    1. Single island -> no article. Example: Ireland is a big island;
    2. Groups of islands -> definite article. Example: the Egadi Islands;
    3. Single Mountain -> no article. Example: Mt. Fuji is Japan's symbol;
    4. Mountain ranges -> definite article. Example: The Alpis are big;
    5. Oceans and Rivers -> definite article. Example: The Pacific Ocean is near the USA;
    6. Lakes -> no article. Example: Lake Garda is big;
  • Acronyms:
    1. Acronyms like FBI, CIA, UN-> definite article. Example: The FBI is investigating;
    2. Acronyms like NATO, NASA -> no article. Example: NASA is working on a project;

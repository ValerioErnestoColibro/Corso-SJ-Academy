English - Lesson 4/11

  • UNREAL CONDITIONALS
    • 2nd Conditional: present/future, hypothetical, contrary to fact, unlikely, if + past simple/continous (would, could, might);
    • Example: If it was/were cold today, I would wear a jacket;
    • Likely vs Unlikely: (L) If I have time tomorrow, I will help you; (U) If I had time tomorrow, I'd help you
    • 3rd Conditional: past, used to complain and criticize, if + past perfect/ppc (would/could/might have + pp)
    • Example: If she had studied more, she would've done better on the test/If you had come earlier, there would've been time to prepare/If you hadn't been using your phone while driving, you wouldn't have had the accident;
  • Mixed Conditional:
    • 2nd + 3rd, past condition, present/future result, If + past perfect/ppc (would, could, might);
    • Example: If he had gotten a university degree, he would make more money;
  • Other unreal structures:
    • Wishes:
      • Present: past simple
      • Past: past perfect
      • About something that bothers you: would
      • Examples: I wish it was Saturday/I wish you were here/I wish a had more money/If only it was Saturday.../I wish you hadn't spent all the money/I wish it hadn't rained on your birthday/I wish they hadn't forgotten the dessert/If only they had remembered the appointment/I wish that dog would stop barking;

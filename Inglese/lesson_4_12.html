English Lesson 4/12 - How to write an ESSAY

  • Argumentative;
  • Formal language;
  • Divided into paragraphs (each paragraph has a singles idea);
  • Use a variety of sentence types (simple, complex, compound);
  • Give both reasons and examples to support your points;
  • Anglo-saxon culture is empirical, we don't like abstract concepts;

  • Structure
  • Introduction (short and direct) must do 2 things: 1) Identify the general topic; 2) State the specific point you want to make about it in a general way;
  • 3 body paragraphs (one for each supporting point);
  • Conclusion (short summary);

  • Examples:
    1. "Many people are afraid that artificial intelligence is going to replace human workers and create a generation of useless people. However, even if machines replace many workers, this will only create more jobs for people as the economy evolves"
    2. "It has been claimed that smart phones have destroyed the intellectual capacity of an entire generation and that young people should be banned from using them. Instead, I think that this generation is developing new capacities that we are not able to appreciate yet"

  • Body paragraph
  • Starts with a "topic sentence" that states the main point of the parahraph (and links it to the previous paragraph). The following sentences provide supporting examples and explanation;
  • Typical beginnings for linked paragraphs:
    1. For points in the same direction: moreover, furthermore, in addition
    2. For contrasting points: however, despite/in spite of, in contrast, on the other hand

  • Conclusion
  • Simple summary, or focus on the significance of this point in a larger context;
  • Examples:
    1. In conclusion, in the end, in short, in summary
    2. "In conclusion, despite the appearance of mental decline, generation A is starting to develop new ways of thinking that older generations lack and are unable to appreciate at the moment"

How to write a Report

  • Reports are divided into paragrahphs/sections, and each section has a Heading;
  • It should have an introduction that explains what is being reported on and give a general idea of what you will discuss;
  • The body should be divided into the different elements being described and give relevant details;
  • It should have a conclusion that makes suggestions or presents a value judgment;

English - Lesson 30/10

  • Modal Auxiliary Verbs (Part 2):
    • OBLIGATION/PROHIBITION (PAST FORMS)
    • STRONG AFFERMATIVE: Had to/Needed to
    • WEAK AFF (ADVICE): Should have + Past Participle;
    • Example: I got a bad mark. I should have studied more.
    • WEAK NEGATIVE (ADVICE): Shouldn't have + Past Participle;
    • Example: I feel tired. I shouldn't have stayed up so late last night.
    • STRONG NEGATIVE: IT WAS PROHIBITED/IT WAS FORBIDDEN;
    • FOR SOMETHING FORBIDDEN BUT DONE ANYWAY, OR SOMETHING OBLIGED BUT NOT DONE: be supposed to;
    • Example: I was supposed to be at work at 8 am, but I didn't go until 10 am/I wasn't supposed to use his computer, but I couldn't resist;
    • NO OBLIGATION: didn'thave to, didn't nedd to = NO OBLIGATION + DIDN'T DO THE ACTION;
    • Needn't have + past participle = NO OBLIGATION BUT DID IT ANYWAY;
    • Example: We needn't have run all the way to the station, the bus was late( = we ran, but it wasn't necessary)/We didn't need to run all the way to the station because we had left early ( = we didn't run because it wasn't necessary);
  • DEDUCTION
    • When you have some information but not all of the facts, and you make a conclusion based on logic, etc.
    • Really sure: must;
    • it's possibile: could, may, might;
    • Really sure it isn't true: can't;
    • Example: His car is parked in front of his building and the lights are on. He must be at home/She hasn't come to work for the last few days. She could have the flu/There is no way that Trump won the election. You can't be serious!
    • Deduction in the past:
      • Really sure it was true: "must have + past participle"
      • Possible true: "could have + past participle/might have + past participle/may have + past participle"
      • Really sure it wasn't true: "can't have + past participle; couldn't have + past participle"
  • Conditionals:
    • Real conditionals: 0, 1st;
    • Unreal conditionals. 2nd, 3rd, mixed;
    • Zero conditional: to express feneral truths or regular/predictable results; if + present simple/present simple;
    • If = When;
    • Example: If you heat water to 100 C° at sea level, it boils/Water boils if you heat it at 100 °C at sea level/If I use my brother's car, he gets furious;
    • FIRST CONDITIONAL: future possibilities, likely to happen; basic form = if + present simple/will; if + any present tense/will-be going to-modals-imperative
    • Example If it rains tomorrow, I'll take my umbrella/She will study harder if the test is next week/If my sister calls during the film, I'm going to be furious/If you've finished eating, I'll get the bill/If you're late, call me/If you're feeling ill, call the doctor/You must study more if you want a good mark
    • Unless = if not;
    • Example: You will get a good mark if you study a lot/You won't get a good mark unless you study a lot;

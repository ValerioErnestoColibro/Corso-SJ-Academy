English - Lesson 29/10

  • Modal auxiliary verbs (part 1):
    • Can
    • Could
    • May
    • Might
    • Must
    • Will
    • Would
    • Should
    • Shall
  • Ability:
    • Can (present/near future);
    • Could (past);
    • Will be able to (distant future);
  • Past ability:
    • General (could/couldn't: When I was a child, I could/couldn't swim)
    • Specific occasion : was able to/couldn't (There was a lot of traffic but I was able to/couldn't arrive in time);
  • Permission: interrogative only, present/future only, Can(informal)/Could(formal)/May(super formal)
  • Requests:
    • Interrogative, present/future, only second person (YOU), Can/Could/Will or Would(Formal and informal) (Can you open the window? Could you please open the window? Will you open the window? Would you please open the window?)
  • Offering:
    • Can I get you a cup of coffee? (Informal);
    • Could I get you a cup of coffee? (Formal);
    • Shall I bring you some tea? (Super formal);
    • Alternative non-modal ways:
      1. Would you like some coffee? (Formal);
      2. Do you want some coffee? (Informal);
      3. How about a coffee? (Informal);
      4. What about having a coffee? (Neutral);
      5. Let's have a coffee (Neutral);
  • Shall:
    • Interrogative only, first person only (I/WE)
    • Offer: Shall I get you some tea? (Formal);
    • Proposal/Suggestion: Shall we go to the cinema after dinner? (Formal);
  • Probability: based on perceived certainty of the speaker:
    • Low certainty: may/might;
    • Moderate certainty: could/can (generalized possibility);
    • High certainty: will, won't (sure it won't happen);
  • Should (expectation of something, rooted in facts = quite sure)
  • Obligation/Prohibition:
    • Strong positive obligations:
    • Must/Need to = personal obligations (moral, personal, etc.)
    • Have to = external obligations (rules, laws, etc.)
    • Weak positive obligation (advice):
    • Should, ought to;
    • No obligation (not necessary):
    • Don't have to;
    • Don't need to;
    • Neddn't + base form;
    • Weak neagtive obligation (advice):
    • Shouldn't;
    • Ought not to;
    • Prohibition:
    • Mustn't;
